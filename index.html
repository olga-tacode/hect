ABOUT ME

Born in Monterrey, Mexico, in 1969, his family moves to Mexico City in 1971, settling in Tlatelolco – where he spends his childhood and teenage years. The Tlatelolco movie theater and its matinees tinge for good his interest in storytelling, which later became a passion and a work. The influence of his elder sister, Elizabeth, who studied at the CUEC and specialized in scriptwriting, drove him to study photography and video at the Coyoacan Active Photography School, where he starts the professional phase of his passion; and where he learns to speak the language of image.

Héctor walks into a set for the first time as the director of photography for Miss Amnesia, a short film by the Iberoamericana University directed by Fernando Moreno, traveling later to Cuba and Chicago to attend scriptwriting and experimental cinema workshops.

Once he returns to Mexico, Héctor founds his own producing house and calls it Cinealquimia, which is born with the project of doing both fiction and documentary cinema and starts with the short film “Solo en Sueños” – which, with its narrative in 35mm cinema and still photo, clearly shows his other passion: Photography.

Héctor currently works as a free-lancer in films, documentaries, institutional cinema, as well as commercial TV for different production companies.
